FAQ

Common Questions

What grade levels do you work with?

I work with students from middle school through early college, covering Pre-Algebra, Algebra I & II, Geometry, Trigonometry, Pre-Calculus, and Calculus I. Whether your child is building foundational skills or preparing for advanced coursework, I tailor each session to their specific level.

Are sessions online or in-person?

Both! I offer flexible online sessions via Zoom with a shared digital whiteboard, as well as in-person sessions at my tutoring space or your home (depending on location). The online setup is highly interactive — students can solve problems in real time just like we're sitting side by side.

How long is a typical session?

Most students thrive with 60-minute sessions, which gives us enough time to review homework, introduce new concepts, and work through practice problems without fatigue. For exam prep or intensive catch-up, I also offer 90-minute blocks. I'll recommend the right duration after our initial consultation.

How often should we meet?

Consistency is key. I typically recommend 1–2 sessions per week. Once a week works well for ongoing support and homework help; twice a week is ideal if your child has a significant gap to close or is preparing for a major exam like the SAT, ACT, or a school final.

What if my child is struggling with exams?

That's exactly what I'm here for. We'll start with a diagnostic session to pinpoint weak areas, then build a focused study plan with targeted practice, past paper walkthroughs, and test-taking strategies. Many of my students see a full letter-grade improvement within 4–6 weeks of focused exam prep.
